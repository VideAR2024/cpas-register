The Dark Target Problem

Thousands of people on water and in low-level airspace are completely invisible to rescue services every day.

When you leave shore, you disappear

Every time a kayaker launches, a paddle boarder heads out, or a paraglider takes flight, they become a Dark Target — invisible to the coastguard, mountain rescue, and every other emergency service.

When something goes wrong, rescue crews are searching blind. It costs critical time, it puts SAR crews at risk, and it costs lives.

Existing solutions require expensive hardware, paid subscriptions, or manual check-in. Most recreational users have neither.

CPAS requires no new hardware — just the phone in your pocket
How CPAS Works

Three steps. Zero hardware.

CPAS — Common Position Awareness System — is a patented technology that turns any mobile phone into a position beacon.

1
📱

Your phone pings

When you head out on the water or into the air, your phone sends a lightweight position ping. No app to download. No subscription.

→
2
📡

CPAS tracks you

Your position appears on a live 3D Digital Twin map visible to rescue services. Updated in real time. Adaptive ping rates based on your activity.

→
3
🆘

Help finds you

If something goes wrong, rescue crews know exactly where you are. No searching blind. No wasted time. Faster response, better outcomes.

The Dark Target Pilot

17+ clubs across five disciplines

The South Coast pilot brings together clubs from Newhaven to Shoreham — sailing, kayaking, SUP, paragliding, open water swimming and fishing — in the UK's first community-powered position awareness trial.

🚣 Kayaking 🏄 SUP ⛵ Sailing 🏊 Open Water Swimming 🎣 Fishing Vessels 🪂 Paragliding 🛩️ Paramotors

Newhaven — Shoreham Corridor

The pilot covers one of the UK's most active coastal corridors for recreational water and air users. Clubs along this stretch are the first to test CPAS in real conditions — proving that a phone ping can replace thousands of pounds of tracking hardware.
